# Full Test Results: CPU vs OpenACC GPU (2026-03-30)

## Overview

Full IQ-TREE ModelFinder + tree reconstruction benchmark comparing 4 workflows:
- **1 CPU** (VANILA) - single-threaded baseline
- **10 CPUs** (OMP_10) - 10 OpenMP threads
- **48 CPUs** (OMP_48) - 48 OpenMP threads (full node)
- **GPU (V100)** (OPENACC) - NVIDIA V100 via OpenACC

**Configuration**: 100 taxa, alignment lengths 100 to 1M sites, seed=1, fixed topology (`-te`)

**100% agreement across all workflows** at all alignment lengths for both DNA and AA:

### 6. GPU Crossover Point

- **DNA**: GPU becomes faster than 48 CPUs at ~100K sites

- **AA**: GPU becomes faster than 1 CPU at ~1K sites

## Observations

1. **Small alignments (100-1K sites)**: GPU overhead dominates. The GPU kernel launch, data transfer, and ModelFinder's many small evaluations make GPU slower than single-threaded CPU. OpenMP 48-thread runs also suffer from thread contention overhead.

2. **Medium alignments (10K sites)**: GPU roughly matches 48 CPUs. Already 7-26x faster than 1 CPU due to massively parallel likelihood evaluation.

3. **Large alignments (100K+ sites)**: GPU clearly wins. At 1M DNA sites, GPU completes in 39 min vs 2.2h for 48 CPUs (3.37x speedup). This is the regime where GPU parallelism over patterns pays off.

4. **DNA vs AA scaling**: GPU speedup is stronger for DNA at large sizes (3.37x vs 1.45x at 100K) because DNA has 4 states while AA has 20, meaning more compute per pattern for AA which keeps CPUs busier.

5. **Thread overhead at tiny data**: 48 CPUs at 100 DNA sites takes 2.4 minutes vs 6.3 seconds for 1 CPU -- a 23x slowdown. Thread synchronization and data partitioning overhead completely dominates useful work.
